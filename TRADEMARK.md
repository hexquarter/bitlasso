# BitLasso Trademark Policy

**Last updated: May 2025**
**Maintained by: HexQuarter**

---

## Overview

The BitLasso name, logo, and associated brand assets are trademarks of HexQuarter
("we", "us", "our"). This policy describes what you may and may not do with these
marks. The goal is to protect the integrity of the BitLasso brand while keeping the
community open and collaborative.

This policy applies independently of the MIT license that governs the BitLasso
frontend source code. An MIT license grants you rights to the code — it does not
grant rights to the BitLasso name or visual identity.

---

## The Marks

The following are protected under this policy:

- The name **"BitLasso"**
- The **BitLasso logo** and logomark
- The domain **bitlasso.xyz** and any subdomains operated by HexQuarter
- Any taglines or slogans explicitly associated with BitLasso by HexQuarter

---

## What You May Do

### ✅ Permitted uses (no permission required)

- Refer to BitLasso by name in articles, blog posts, talks, documentation, or
  research — for example: *"built with BitLasso"* or *"compatible with BitLasso"*
- State clearly and accurately that your project is a fork of BitLasso — for
  example: *"MyApp is a fork of the BitLasso frontend"*
- Use the BitLasso name when contributing to this repository or discussing the
  project in community spaces
- Use the name in academic or journalistic contexts to refer to the project

### ✅ Permitted with attribution

- Include the BitLasso name in comparisons or evaluations, provided the
  comparison is accurate and not misleading

---

## What You May Not Do

### ❌ Not permitted without explicit written permission

- Use **"BitLasso"** as the name of a forked project, derivative product, hosted
  service, or commercial offering — even if the underlying code is MIT-licensed
- Use the BitLasso logo or any confusingly similar logo in your project,
  product, or marketing materials
- Register domain names, social handles, or app store listings using "BitLasso"
  or confusingly similar variations (e.g. *bitlasso-app*, *getbitlasso*,
  *bitlassoapp*)
- Imply official affiliation with, endorsement by, or sponsorship from HexQuarter
  without written consent
- Use the BitLasso name in a way that could damage the reputation of the project
  or mislead users about the source of a product

---

## Forks and Derivatives

You are free to fork and modify the BitLasso frontend under the terms of the MIT
license. However, any publicly distributed fork or derivative must:

1. Use a **clearly distinct name** — not "BitLasso" or any name likely to be
   confused with it
2. Include a notice in the README stating the project is derived from BitLasso
   — for example: *"This project is based on BitLasso (bitlasso.xyz)"*
3. Not use the BitLasso logo or visual identity without permission

If you would like to use the BitLasso name or logo in a way not covered above,
reach out to us at **hello@hexquarter.com** with a brief description of your
intended use. We review requests case by case and aim to respond within 14 days.

## Enforcement

We reserve the right to revoke permission for use of the BitLasso marks at any
time if we determine the use is harmful, misleading, or in violation of this
policy. We will generally attempt to resolve issues informally before taking
formal action.

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be noted in
the repository commit history. Continued use of the marks following a policy
update constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.
